Chocolate Pizza

Posted on 15 DEC 2013/DESSERTS

To make the chocolate pizza base, follow the instructions in the first paragraph of the previous answer. While the pizza crust is baking, you can prepare the fig swirl. In a small saucepan, combine diced figs, honey, and water. Cook the mixture over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the figs are soft and the liquid has thickened to a syrupy consistency. Remove the fig swirl from the heat and set it aside to cool. Once the pizza crust is finished baking, remove it from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes. Then, spread a layer of melted chocolate over the pizza crust, leaving a small border around the edge. Drizzle the fig swirl over the top of the chocolate layer in a spiral pattern. Use a toothpick or knife to gently swirl the fig mixture into the chocolate layer. Place the chocolate pizza back in the oven and bake for an additional 5-7 minutes until the chocolate is melted and the fig swirl is lightly browned. Remove the pizza from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before slicing. Serve the chocolate pizza with a scoop of vanilla ice cream on top and a drizzle of extra fig swirl, if desired. Enjoy!

1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour 1/4 cup granulated sugar 1/4 teaspoon salt 1 package (2 1/4 teaspoons) active dry yeast 2/3 cup warm water 1/2 cup diced dried figs 1/4 cup honey 1/4 cup water 1/2 cup semisweet chocolate chips, melted Vanilla ice cream, for serving Note: You may also need a little extra flour for dusting the work surface and rolling out the pizza dough.
